Introduction to Mitochondria

Popularly known as the “Powerhouse of the cell,” mitochondria (singular: mitochondrion) are a double membrane-bound organelle found in most eukaryotic organisms. They are found inside the cytoplasm and essentially function as the cell’s “digestive system.”

They play a major role in breaking down nutrients and generating energy-rich molecules for the cell. Many of the biochemical reactions involved in cellular respiration take place within the mitochondria. The term ‘mitochondrion’ is derived from the Greek words

  • “mitos” and
  • “chondrion”

which means

  • “thread” and
  • “granules-like”

respectively. It was first described by a German pathologist named Richard Altmann in the year 1890.
